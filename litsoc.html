"Choices
The sound of breaking glass shatters me out of my reverie. I drop the sandwich, the plate bouncing against the floor. The taste of sweet tea turns sour in my mouth. His foul breath blows against my face, the slurred words, the rough shoves. Bruises bloom purple. I look down at my arm, track marks not quite faded, lines I wish were older, and a ring I have yet to take off. A roadmap of poor choices, for fleeting moments of ecstasy. I feel a phantom touch on my palm, soft blond hair, bright blue eyes. Eyes not dim, bruises not bloomed. I protect her mind and body; she protects my soul. I clutch at the memory as I see his approaching form. His voice is getting louder. I step back. I hold tight to my consciousness. My hands are hidden. It's dark. Light from the flickering bulbs glints on the knife's edge as I sink it into his chest. He chokes on his blood, eyes going wide. As he wilts, I see her in my mind's eye. The pills are working now. "With a broken promise,love to rots
My mother is gracious; healing smile and tender hands. Her skin was soft and smooth before she had me. With the memory of every injustice she's been handed, sinking deeper beneath her cracked skin, betrayal aches in her bones. I never sleep well; I fear she gently settles on my bed every night, hovering over me with a knife against my throat. No drink of water nor bite of food will go past my mouth without the look of desperation in my eyes, ""Please, please have not poisoned this."" By Cod do I know how strong women are. If I were my mother, I would want to kill my child, too. It is with every one of my careless errors that she realizes time and time again, ""I do not deserve this."" Raised with the promise of love, every hand on her must feel like claws prying a fresh wound. Every word must sound like a corkscrew twirling into her neck. For a woman with enough pent-up rage and horror in her to burn the world to ashes, it is fair for her to tighten her hands around my throat with each disintegrating thought. ""You are a curse to this house."" ""I know, ma."" As afraid as I am that you will finally cross the line, stain your cooking knife with my blood; receiving a well-deserved catharsis, you never will. Though you'll never say it, your eyes always search for forgiveness in mine.

"The Walk Home
Icarus died because he flew too close to the sun. People consider it as a tragedy, but I would say it was mercy. What if he was still falling from the sky, suspended in a place between life and death experiencing the fear, the agony, the pain repeatedly? In anticipation of what'll come next, he would still be waiting for an inevitable fate that doesn't arrive. But there is another question that arises. What if he had survived? What would have become of him then? Would he have gone mad or would this have been a key for him to transcend to another level? We can only form conjectures. The possibilities of what could have been are infinite. With these thoughts on my mind on the way to my house I saw something. A stroller, with a baby, rolling down the road about to crash. With supernatural reflexes I had no prior idea about I managed to pull the baby out of the stroller. The moment I saw the baby I was holding, something happened. A butterfly fluttered its wing, the first domino fell, the first reaction started. Then it looked at me, the ripple started, the second domino dropped, the chain continued. Then he smiled. The ripple turned into tsunami, dominoes fell in heap, the chain progressed exponentially. I was reduced to an entangled mess of feelings. He was a strange creature. Moments ago, facing death and now, smiling. His mother came to thank me but I was in a daze. The feeling of his touch still imprinted on me and my soul. Maybe something major shifted inside the universe in that small tiny moment because it seemed different somehow. A smile, probably first in a long time, creeped across my face. And then I continued my walk home.
Amit Anand "

"Mountain of Guilt
I don't even remember the first time I saw her. Maybe it wasn't that important for me back then. Yeah, she was like that. She would disappear in front of you and you won't even notice. They said she was a loner ; a freak. She was tagged a 'loser'. Losers are those who lack courage; but little did they know that she was the bravest soul among us all. What she really needed was a helping hand, someone she could rely on in her lows: a friend. Was it so difficult to make a friend? I remember the day she slipped and fell face-first on the corridor floor. I was right there. She looked around, as if hoping someone would help her and her eyes met mine. It felt as though she knew I would help, I really wanted to. Yet, I stood there like a stone. Afraid that my friends would call me a freak too. She was laughed at. Tears streamed down her beautiful face. Every time our eyes met, they beseeched me. One warm hug, a mouthful of words would suffice to soothe her. But I did nothing. Today, I heard she attempted suicide. My heart sank Tears came out as if they wanted to punish me. Was my ego more important than her life? Disgust rose deep inside me. What had I done? How did I become this pathetic? Was it really her who deserved to die? I could have killed myself that day and felt nothing. You never know what someone is going through. Even a few words can make a difference.
Yash Tlppanawar "

"You Never Know!
I never forget a face! This is the claim I Often make, priding my ability to recognize people I've encountered. However, names and circumstances evade my memory, a fact that recently led me into an unforeseen predicament. Just the other day, I found myself at a cafe, eagerly awaiting a childhood friend I hadn't seen in 15 years. As I waited, my eyes were drawn to a pale, thin man by the corner. His face looked strangely familiar, triggering a growing suspicion, could this be my long-lost buddy? Driven by curiosity, I approached and sat across from him, initiating a conversation. The man mostly listened as I spoke, wearing a warm smile on his oval face, reminiscent of the person I remembered from all those years ago. He mentioned losing his job and seeking new employment, but divulged nothing about his family or residence. I brushed it off, assuming his shyness around relatively less familiar faces persisted. He hadn't changed a bit. As we prepared to leave, rain suddenly poured down. He hesitantly asked if I could drop him by the junction, to which I playfully teased him and agreed to give him a lift. We had merely reached the junction when he asked me to take a turn at the by- road. He simply got down in the rain and I too got down to bring him back. I was attacked and the next thing I realise was that my car and purse were gone. I was left in a state of shock, realizing I had fallen victim to a clever impostor. The following day, I mustered the courage to visit the police station and report the incident. In the midst of the commotion, that a particular poster caught my eye: ""Wanted,"" it read. To my dismay, the face staring back at me was none other than the imposter who had cunningly deceived me!
Laavanya Rajan "

Manga

Let your imagination expand its wings, soaring through the colorful skies of artistic creativity and bubbling passion for a good tale. The beautifully detailed panels of mangas gasp out tales as intricate as the brief blooming of cherry blossoms which melt before our eyes to the stories. Where heroes and heroines our favorite of them jump out of the darkness, their tales painting shades of bravery and magic into our minds unknowingly molding us into one of them. Like a river slowly chipping away rocks to make its way through, so are the stories found in the manga that sculpt our hearts diving into our humanity making the world a better place to live. The Manga Club is a world where each panel is an entire universe unto itself, every single one of them.

Quizzing & Debating

Our mind is a sculptor, chiseling away the marble to reveal the stunning masterpiece of knowledge and eloquence within us. Quizzing is not just about intelligence, it is like a key, carefully unlocking the simplest of puzzles, transforming everyday facts into treasures of insight. Debate, on the other hand, is a symphony where confidence is the conductor, orchestrating a melody of words that resonate with power and precision. It’s not just what we say, but how we say it – each argument is a brushstroke on the canvas of war. Together, quizzing and debating are the forge and flame, crafting minds as resilient and radiant as the sun.
